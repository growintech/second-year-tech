1
Choose your track and analyse the problem
Individual + pair discussion
⏱ ~20 min

Browse the 5 tracks on the overview page. Read each one carefully — the context, the real problem, and the stimulus questions. Discuss with your partner and choose the one that feels most relevant or interesting to you.

Once you have chosen, build your Problem Tree using XMind, Miro, or pen and paper. Your tree must:

  • Start from the core problem defined in the track
  • Break it down into at least 2–3 root causes
  • Lead naturally to 2–3 possible solutions

Watch the tutorial first if you haven't done one before, and look at the startup examples for reference.

2
Define your target audience
Pair discussion
⏱ ~10 min

Based on your Problem Tree, define your target audience as specifically as possible. A good target is not "young people" — it is a real person with a real, specific problem.

✅ Good example: "A 17-year-old student in Milan who takes two buses to school every morning and misses the first class at least once a week because of delays."
❌ Too vague: "Young people who have trouble with public transport."

Write your target definition down — you will need to submit it alongside your Problem Tree.

3
Pick one solution and write the Elevator Pitch
Pair work
⏱ ~15 min

From the 2–3 solutions at the bottom of your Problem Tree, choose the strongest one. Ask yourself: which solution is most original, most feasible, and most clearly connected to the root causes you found?

Then write your Elevator Pitch — maximum 10 lines, answering three questions:

  1. What does the product do?
  2. Who uses it? (your target audience)
  3. What makes it different from what already exists?

Give your startup a name. Provisional is fine — you can change it later.

💡 Problem statement format. Your pitch must start with a clear problem statement — a single sentence that explains the issue. "Students waste 30 minutes a day waiting for late buses with no reliable information" — not "we built an app for commuters".
