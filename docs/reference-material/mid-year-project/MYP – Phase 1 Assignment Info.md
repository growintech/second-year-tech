# **Mid Year Project – Phase 1**

During today’s class, you will start working on the problem your startup will solve.

### 1️⃣ Step 1 – Choose the problem (in class)

During today’s lesson, choose (or take inspiration from) one problem from the Problem Tracks appendix. The [**Problem Tracks**](https://docs.google.com/document/u/0/d/169Ym6hzLAblhq8kP8lSNwx-nj_sotAcaD9UYwsVfPPI/edit) are meant to give you a head start and help you identify a strong, real-world problem.  
By the end of today’s lesson, your group must have clearly chosen the problem you want to work on.

### 2️⃣ Step 2 – Build the Problem Tree

After choosing the problem, analyze it using the Problem Tree method ([video attached](https://www.loom.com/share/70c6057d71804a31b80c1e556cf60462)).  
Your Problem Tree must lead to at least 2–3 possible solutions. This step is meant to help you explore the problem in depth before committing to a single solution. You can either use XMind, Miro or similar tools for this task. 

### 3️⃣ Step 3 – Choose one solution and create a feedback form

From the 2–3 solutions identified in your Problem Tree, choose one.  
Create a feedback form for this solution. The form must contain at least 3 questions aimed at understanding whether users would be interested in your solution. Don’t worry, we made [5 complete startup examples](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1hgVrsJcedr0YYf8Uj0D5F3D66FXvJRKHmBa6huLaQ4U/edit?usp=sharing) (with forms\!\!) for you to take inspiration from.

### 4️⃣ Step 4 – Assign startup roles

Your group must assign roles (for example: CEO, Developer, Designer). These roles are very important for the next phase of the project.
