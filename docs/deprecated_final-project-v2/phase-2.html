✋ Checkpoint — before you start

At the beginning of this session, each pair will have 5 minutes to briefly present to the instructor:

  1. Your problem statement (one clear sentence)
  2. Your Problem Tree — the root causes and solutions you found
  3. The solution you chose and why
  4. Your startup name

This checkpoint is not graded — it's a chance to get feedback before you go deeper. Come prepared.

1
Build the validation form
Pair work
⏱ ~20 min

Create a form using Google Forms or Tally to test whether other people feel the problem and would use your solution. You need to collect at least 20 responses before the deadline.

Read the how-to guide below before you start writing questions — a badly structured form will give you useless data.

  • Ask about the problem, not the solution — at least at first.
    ❌ "Would you use an app that helps you manage your money?"
    ✅ "How often do you reach the end of the month without knowing how you spent your money?"
  • Use numerical scales (1–5 or 1–10) for intensity questions — they give you quantifiable data.
  • Include at least one open question — open answers surface insights you'd never have thought to ask for.
  • Briefly explain your idea before asking if people would use it — 2–3 lines about your startup, then ask.
  • Keep it under 8–10 questions — beyond that, people abandon the form or answer randomly.

You can also look at the startup examples document ↗ — it includes complete form examples for 5 different startups.

Once you have collected 20+ responses, write a short comment (5–10 lines) on what you learned: were your assumptions confirmed? Did anything surprise you? How will this shape your product?

2
Build the Pitch Deck (4 slides)
Pair work
⏱ ~30 min

Create a presentation on Google Slides or Canva. Your deck must have exactly these 4 slides:

Slide 1
Problem & Target — your problem statement (one sentence) and who it affects. Make the problem feel real and urgent.
Slide 2
Solution — what your product does. One clear sentence + a brief description. Show how it directly addresses the root causes in your Problem Tree.
Slide 3
Market & Competitors — TAM, SAM and SOM data with sources, plus 1–2 competitors. Every number must have a source. Use PerplexityAI Deep Research to find the data — see the how-to guide below.
Slide 4
Call to Action / Conclusion — why your idea wins. What makes it different? What is the one thing you want the audience to remember?

Use PerplexityAI Deep Research with a precise prompt to get numerical TAM/SAM/SOM data with verifiable sources. Every number you put in the deck must have a source — PerplexityAI provides them automatically.

Find the full ready-to-use template in the Deep Research prompt document ↗. Take the key numbers and their sources and insert them directly into Slide 3.
