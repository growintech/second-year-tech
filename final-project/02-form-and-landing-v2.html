Form & Landing Page

Prove that the problem exists with real data, then build the first version of your startup website.

Step 1Market Validation Form

Create the form

Use Google Forms or Tally to create a validation survey. The goal is to understand whether other people actually feel the problem you identified in Phase 1.

  • 1
    Start with the problem — not the solution
    Ask about the person's experience with the problem before you mention your idea. Example: "How often do you feel you've lost control of your screen time?" — not "Would you use our app?"
  • 2
    Use scales and multiple-choice questions
    Numerical scales (1–5) give you quantifiable data you can cite in the pitch deck. Mix them with a few multiple-choice questions for variety.
  • 3
    Include at least one open question
    Open answers often reveal insights you never thought to ask about. Something like: "What's the most frustrating thing about [topic]?"
  • 4
    Introduce your idea, then ask about it
    Write 2–3 lines explaining your startup concept, then ask: "Would you use something like this? Why or why not?"
  • 5
    Collect at least 20 responses
    Share the form with classmates, friends, family. The more diverse the respondents, the better. 20 is the minimum.
💡
Keep it short. Stay under 8–10 questions. Beyond that, people abandon the form or answer randomly — and your data becomes useless.

Analyse the results

Once you have your 20+ responses, write a brief comment (5–10 lines) on what you learned. Focus on:

  • Did people confirm the problem exists?
  • Were there any surprises in the responses?
  • How many said they would use your solution?
  • Did you change anything about your idea based on the feedback?

Take screenshots of the key charts from Google Forms / Tally — you'll need them for the delivery and for your pitch deck later.

Step 2First Website (Landing Page)

Build the first version of your startup site

This is the first delivery of your website. It doesn't need to be complete, but it must demonstrate that you can build a real page with HTML, CSS and JavaScript.

  • 1
    Responsive layout
    The page must look good on both desktop and mobile. Use CSS media queries or a framework like Bootstrap.
  • 2
    Working navbar
    A navigation bar at the top of the page that links to different sections. It should be responsive (hamburger menu on mobile is a plus, but not required).
  • 3
    Clear sections
    The page should describe your startup clearly: the problem, the solution, how it works, and who it's for. Think of it as a real startup landing page.
🚀
Don't wait for perfection. The website evolves over three deliveries. This first version is about structure and content. You'll add the countdown and interactive features in Phase 3.

Start the development diary

Create a Google Doc and start documenting your process as you build the website. This is the first of three diary deliveries.

  • What you tried to do
  • What tools or techniques you used
  • Problems you ran into and how you solved them
  • If you used AI: what prompt you gave, what it produced, what you changed
