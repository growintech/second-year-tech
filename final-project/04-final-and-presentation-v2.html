🎤 Presentation Day — 22 / 24 April

This is the last lesson of the course. You will present your startup in front of the class and instructors. This is your moment to show everything you've built.

The Presentation — 5 to 7 Minutes

Structure your talk

You have 5–7 minutes to present. Use the pitch deck as visual support — do not read from the slides. Structure your talk in three clear parts:

  • 1
    Problem → Solution → Market
    Walk through your pitch deck. Present the problem you identified, your solution, the validation data from the form, and your market research (TAM/SAM/SOM, competitors). Make it compelling — you're pitching an idea.
  • 2
    Live website demo
    Open your website and show it running live. Demonstrate the countdown, the interactive feature, the responsive layout. Be ready to explain the code behind each feature.
  • 3
    Call to action + Q&A
    Close with a strong statement about why your startup idea wins. Then take questions from the instructors — they may ask about your code, your design choices, or your market analysis.

Presentation tips

  • Rehearse out loud at least twice — timing yourself. 5 minutes goes faster than you think.
  • Split the talk between both partners. Both of you must speak. Decide in advance who covers which section.
  • Test the website before presentation day. Open it on the classroom computer, make sure nothing is broken.
  • If they ask about your code — explain the logic step by step. Don't panic. If you built it (or studied the AI-generated code), you'll be fine.
  • Don't read from slides. Use them as visual support. Look at the audience, not the screen.
