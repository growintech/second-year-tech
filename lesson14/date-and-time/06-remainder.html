📝 Key Concept

The
remainder pattern

The most important pattern before building the countdown. Work through the arithmetic on paper first — then verify it in code below.

The problem

Why can't we just divide?

We have a difference of 7,263,847,000 ms and want to know: how many days, hours, minutes, seconds is that?

Dividing the full milliseconds by 3,600,000 gives over 2,000 hours — not the 1 hour that makes sense.

The key insight: each unit lives inside the remainder left after extracting the previous unit.

Work through it — step by step

Starting with 7,263,847,000 ms. Calculate each answer before revealing it.

Step 1 — How many whole days?

There are 86,400,000 ms in one day.

7,263,847,000 ÷ 86,400,000 = ? How many whole days?
84.07... → parseInt → 84 days
How many ms do those 84 days take up?
84 × 86,400,000 = 7,257,600,000 ms
How many ms are left over (the remainder)?
7,263,847,000 − 7,257,600,000 = 6,247,000 ms

Step 2 — How many whole hours? (from the remainder)

There are 3,600,000 ms in one hour. Work with the remainder: 6,247,000 ms.

6,247,000 ÷ 3,600,000 = ? How many whole hours?
1.73... → parseInt → 1 hour
How many ms are left after removing that 1 hour?
6,247,000 − (1 × 3,600,000) = 2,647,000 ms

Step 3 — How many whole minutes?

There are 60,000 ms in one minute. Remainder is now 2,647,000 ms.

2,647,000 ÷ 60,000 = ? How many whole minutes?
44.1... → parseInt → 44 minutes
How many ms are left?
2,647,000 − (44 × 60,000) = 7,000 ms

Step 4 — How many seconds?

There are 1,000 ms in one second. Remainder: 7,000 ms.

7,000 ÷ 1,000 = ? How many seconds?
7 seconds

Final answer

✅
7,263,847,000 ms = 84 days, 1 hour, 44 minutes, 7 seconds The pattern: extract the biggest unit → subtract what you used → repeat with the remainder.
