Sometimes text is hidden by making its color match the background. The DevTools inspector (F12 → Elements tab) shows all the HTML, including hidden content. Look at the paragraph inside Document #2 — check its color and background CSS properties.

🔓 Challenge solved!

Hiding text by matching its color to the background is called "invisible ink" and provides zero security. The text exists in the DOM, can be selected, and shows up instantly in DevTools. Attackers (and search engines) read DOM, not pixels.

The lesson: If you don't want users to see data, don't send it to the browser at all.
